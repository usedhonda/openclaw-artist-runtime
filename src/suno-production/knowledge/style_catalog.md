# Style Catalog

These templates are short-tag starting points. Builders should replace bracketed
values and keep the core style compact. Do not copy artist names, song titles,
or album titles into Style.

## Tag Order

`genre, BPM, key, mood, vocal, instruments, mix`

Optional performance directions can follow on new lines:

`Verse: <delivery and density>`

`Chorus: <lift and instrumentation>`

## Nu-Jazz Rap

Core:

`nu-jazz rap, [BPM] BPM, [key], sardonic tension, dry male vocal, Rhodes, electric bass, horn stabs, basement mix`

Exclude:

`trap hats, glossy pop synths, sax solo, autotune`

Use when the lyric depends on social observation, satire, or spoken density.

## Alternative Pop

Core:

`alternative pop, [BPM] BPM, [key], wounded glow, intimate vocal, cold synths, restrained drums, warm low end`

Exclude:

`stadium rock, EDM drop, choir pad`

Use for reflective songs that need melody without over-polish.

## Synthwave / Dark Electronic

Core:

`dark synthwave, [BPM] BPM, [key], night drive, controlled vocal, analog bass, arpeggio synths, neon room`

Exclude:

`dubstep wobble, harsh risers, random glitches`

Use for urban motion, surveillance, memory, or late-night imagery.

## EDM / Dance

Core:

`melodic house, [BPM] BPM, [key], rising release, clean vocal chop, deep kick, sidechain bass, wide club mix`

Exclude:

`muddy low end, brittle hats, crowd noise`

Use short lyric phrases and clear build/drop tags.

## Folk / Acoustic

Core:

`modern folk, [BPM] BPM, [key], close confession, soft vocal, fingerpicked guitar, upright piano, dry room`

Exclude:

`arena drums, synth lead, heavy distortion`

Use when lyrics carry the story and the arrangement should stay transparent.

## Rock / Post-Punk

Core:

`post-punk rock, [BPM] BPM, [key], angular pressure, raw vocal, choppy guitars, driving bass, dry drums`

Exclude:

`metal solo, glossy pop chorus, trap percussion`

Use for blunt statements, repeated slogans, or city-night tension.

## Ballad / R&B

Core:

`slow R&B ballad, [BPM] BPM, [key], aching restraint, breathy vocal, electric piano, soft bass, close mix`

Exclude:

`power metal drums, EDM riser, novelty voice`

Use longer vowels, fewer words, and a final chorus that opens emotionally.

## Instrumental Cue

Core:

`instrumental ambient pop, [BPM] BPM, [key], suspended calm, no lead vocal, soft pads, piano motif, wide reverb`

Exclude:

`spoken words, lead vocal, harsh percussion`

Use only when the payload is marked instrumental.

## Performance Direction Examples

Keep these short:

```text
Verse: close and restrained, rhythm forward
Chorus: wider, brighter, hook emphasized
Bridge: stripped to keys and voice
Outro: short, resolved, no loop
```
